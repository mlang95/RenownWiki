Diplomacy Actions

1. Sign Treaty

Cost: None

If passes, ask other players to agree to sign a treaty. Choose one agreeing player and both players sign one of the following treaties:

  • Peace Treaty: End war between you and the target player & Set a truce timer 5.
  • Trade Agreement: Begin trading with the target player (Trade Income flows beginning next Turn — see Trade rules).
  • Non-Aggression Pact: Neither player may declare war on the other while the NAP is in effect. If the NAP is later ended via end treaty, both players gain a truce timer 5.
  • Alliance: join, form, or invite a player to a defensive or Military Alliance (see Alliance Rules).

Endorsed: perform a Diplomacy action.

Note: if acting on behalf of an alliance you are a member of, end treaty requires all allies to agree.

Note: a player may not be in more than one alliance at a time (exception: master of duplicity faction).

2. Negotiate

Cost: None

If passes, propose terms to a target player. The target player chooses to agree or refuse. If they agree, both players are bound to the agreed terms.

Negotiate terms may include:

  • Gold transfers
  • Settlement ownership transfers
  • Territory transfers
  • Treaty signing or ending (any Treaty type from Diplomacy)
  • Promises (non-binding intent — see note below)

Endorsed: perform a Diplomacy action.

Note: promises cannot be enforced by the rules. However, if a player fails to fulfill a promise they agreed to in a negotiate, the affected player may perform a declare war action targeting that player.

Note: negotiate is also used to resolve demand tribute and negotiate a surrender during sieges. In those contexts, the negotiate is automatic and does not require a diplomacy envoy.

3. End Treaty

Cost: None

If passes, choose a target with an active treaty signed with you. That treaty is ended.

End Treaty: Choose an existing treaty between you and a target player or target alliance. The treaty is removed from play. End treaty does not require the target player to agree.

Endorsed: perform a Diplomacy action.
