Escalation — Battle Rules

Dice Principle

A successful roll is written X+: roll that number or higher on a d6. Each −1 penalty requires your roll to be one higher; each +1 bonus, one lower. If a 7+ is ever needed it automatically fails; if a 1 or less is needed it automatically passes (but if a natural result could still trigger an effect, you still roll). A natural roll is the die before modifiers; a natural 6 triggers Cleave, Deadly, Destroy Shield and Riposte (and a natural-6 Save fails against Poison).

Setup

Each player starts with 25 Levy retinues, Farm Tools, Cloth armor, no shield. All four Domains start Untested. No Pursuits, 0 VP. Every Battle is fought at an army size of 25; Standings, Pursuits, unlocks and VP carry over.

Turn Sequence

1 — Battle. Pair off and fight one Battle (rotate pairings each turn).

ResultVP
Decisive Win: reduce the enemy army to zero or cause a Rout.+3
Minor Victory: the enemy army Fell Back.+2
Lose, but end it via a successful Fall Back with 1+ retinue remaining.+1
Lose any other way (wiped out, Routed, failed Fall Back).0

2 — Advancement (all players simultaneously): unlock one Domain Standing (advance one Domain one step, if the prior standing is unlocked); perform one Build Action (construct one Pursuit you qualify for); and you may change your army to any composition you have unlocked.

Battle Walkthrough

Roll off for Initiative (+1 if you won your last Battle, re-roll ties). The higher roller Seizes the Initiative: they are the Attacker and gain +1 Initiative in the first Skirmish. A Battle is a series of Skirmishes — repeat these steps until it ends:

  1. Form the Field. Place up to 10 retinues in the front line (one attack each) plus up to 5 in reserve.
  2. Choose Tactics. Both players secretly pick one Tactic, then reveal together.
  3. Declare equipment. The Attacker names equipment first; the Defender responds.
  4. Initiative. Runs −2 to +2. Higher Strikes first. At −2 or lower you Blunder (Strike can't be improved beyond 6+).
  5. Roll to Strike. Roll a d6 per front-line retinue against the to-Strike number (plus bonuses, minus 1 per Fatigue token, to a max of 6+, then other penalties).
  6. Strike and defend. Resolve Strikes — Parry (resolve Ripostes), then Save, then Recover. Casualties leave the field at once.
  7. Panic check. A side taking 5+ casualties this Skirmish takes a Panic check (d6 per retinue, up to 5 dice); a value modified to 7+ Routs the army. At most once per Skirmish.
  8. Strike back. The other player Strikes the same way, if able.
  9. Lose Endurance. Each army that fought loses 1 Endurance; at 0 it is Fatigued.
  10. Break check. Each Fatigued field rolls Morale (up to 5 dice) before gaining its token; failures are casualties; never triggers Panic. 7+ Routs.
  11. Fatigue token. Each Fatigued side gains a token: −1 to Strike, Morale, Parry, Recover (capped 6+ except Morale); they stack.
  12. End the Skirmish. The Battle ends if a side hits 0, Routs, or Falls Back. You cannot Fall Back in the first two Skirmishes.
