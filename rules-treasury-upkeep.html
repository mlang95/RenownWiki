Treasury & Upkeep

Your Treasury is the gold you control. Each Empire Phase you gain your Revenue (tax, trade, and pursuit income) and pay your Upkeep (armies, pursuits, and infrastructure); the net lands in your Treasury. Costs you pay during the turn come out of the same Treasury.

Tracking tip: keep three small piles — Treasury, Revenue, Upkeep. When you build something with income or upkeep, add it to the matching pile; when it's damaged or an army loses retinues, subtract it. Then each turn it's just Treasury + Revenue − Upkeep, with no recounting.

Insolvency & Bankruptcy

> This is an exception path — skip it until it happens.

You go Insolvent the moment your Treasury can't cover a cost in full (at Upkeep, when paying for an action, or when an effect drains your gold). Pay what you can, then cut until the books balance, in this order:

  • Disband retinues, one at a time, cheapest-upkeep first, until your armies are gone.
  • Set buildings inactive, highest-tier first (Monuments, then Power pursuits, then other non-natural pursuits).

An inactive pursuit gives no innate or mastery effect, and anything that needed it as a prerequisite loses its mastery. An inactive piece is restored at any future Upkeep Step where you can pay its upkeep. Build Timers halt while you are Insolvent.

If you've cut everything and a debt still remains, you are Bankrupt: your Treasury goes negative by the unpaid amount (a Debt Marker). While Bankrupt you cannot spend gold or be Extorted, and Build Timers stay halted; Revenue pays down the debt first. When your Treasury reaches 0 or above, Bankruptcy ends. (If you are Vassalized while Bankrupt, the debt is forgiven and your Suzerain takes your Treasury, to a minimum of 0.)
