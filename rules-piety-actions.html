Piety Actions

1. Spread Gospel

Cost: doubt 1

If passes, all non-allied players gain doubt 1. All allied players gain faith 1.

Endorsed: Gain faith 1

2. Send Missionaries

Cost: doubt 1

If passes, choose a target player or alliance.

  • Outside your Alliance: All players in the target alliance gain doubt 2.
  • Inside your Alliance: All other allied players gain faith 2.

Endorsed: Gain faith 1.

3. Tithe

Cost: doubt 1

If passes, choose another player. Extort 10% of their treasury (rounded down to the nearest 100, minimum 0).

Endorsed: Gain faith 1.

4. Convert

Cost: doubt 1

If passes, choose another player’s closest non-capital settlement. The target player who controls the settlement must have public order of −5 or lower.

Perform a lay siege action on the target settlement using only settlement-type siege modifiers (Wooden Walls, Stone Walls, Citadel, Garrison, Standing Army), and set a convert timer.

When the convert timer reaches 0: the target settlement becomes part of your empire (see Capture rules).

Endorsed: Gain faith 1.

Note: if the target player’s public order changes to 1 or higher before the convert timer increments to 0, the convert fails and the timer is removed from play.

5. Crusade

Cost: Doubt 1, requires Sovereign Piety

If passes, declare war on a target player who is not an ally, not under a Non-Aggression Pact, and not under a truce timer. Then immediately perform a move action with one of your armies.

Endorsed: Gain faith 1.

Note: while a crusade is active, neither the crusader nor the target player may perform declare war, sign treaty, end treaty, negotiate, or demand tribute actions targeting each other.

Note: a player may only be on one active crusade at a time. A crusade ends only when one of the players involved becomes vassalized.
