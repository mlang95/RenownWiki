Core Principles

  • Granted Authority: Any mechanic not explicitly described does not function.
  • Special rule priority
  • More specific rules override general rules.
  • Example: Inquisitorial Palace ≥ Established Cunning > General Cunning Rule.
  • Public information
  • All information is public: resources, domains, Treaties, Armies, Pursuits, and Garrisons are known to all players.
  • Once Per Turn Effects cannot be used on the same target.
  • Innate + Mastery = Cumulative.
  • All pursuits activate effects when resolved.
  • A Damaged Pursuit or Infrastructure has neither innate nor mastery effects active.
  • Repair to restore.
  • Timer 0
  • All timers whose initial value is 0 or less resolve immediately.
  • Dice rules
  • If a Roll is modified beyond 6+, it automatically fails.
  • If a Roll is modified less than 2+, it automatically passes.
  • If modified to 1+ or 7+ but specific results trigger on a 1 or 6, dice must still be rolled for those specific results.
  • You may not re-roll a re-roll.
  • Effects that occur on a 1 or 6 occur on unmodified rolls of 1 or 6.
  • Fate rolling
  • Roll 2D6: double sixes = always success; double ones = always failure.
  • Rounding down / denomination of 100
  • Whenever a gold amount is not divisible by 100, round down to the nearest 100, minimum 0.
  • if your Treasury is negative, round up to the nearest 100 instead.
  • PEMDAS rule
  • Whenever adding and multiplying: add all values first, then multiply. If multiple things multiply against the same sum, add the multiplication values together first.
  • Transitive property
  • The transitive property states that if one value equals a second, and the second equals a third, then the first and third must be equal.
  • Negative values (see Glossary).
  • Range(x)
  • Range is the number of movement required to reach a specific territory. For example, Range 5 means an army would move 5 territories to go from inside one settlement to another.
  • Influence Spending: On a single vote, you may spend influence equal to 1 + your domain Standing in that domain (Untested = 1, Rising = 2, Established = 3, Sovereign = 4).
  • Ward exemption only applies when in the same settlement ward with efficient.
  • Monument Pursuits are unique unless started with a Faction.
