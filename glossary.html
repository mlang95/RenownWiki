Glossary

-1 to Parry
A stacking -1 penalty to the defender's Parry roll (to a maximum of 6+). Sources: Unstoppable, Deflect, and each Fatigue token.
2H
Cannot use a Shield.
AP
Armor Penetration — a weapon's (negative) modifier to the defender's Save roll; the more negative, the harder to save.
Abstain
Decline to spend Influence on a vote.
Alliance
A Treaty binding players to mutual support; Military and Defensive Alliances unlock by Era. → in context
Attacker / Defender
Set by the roll-off. Each Skirmish the Attacker declares equipment first; the Defender then responds.
Bandit
Neutral hostile force; Bandit Camps spawn in Outlaw Country and on low Public Order. → in context
Battle
One fight between two players, resolved as a series of Skirmishes until a side is wiped out, Routs, or Falls Back.
Blocked
-1 Initiative in the first Skirmish (negated by Immune Blocked).
Blunder
At Initiative -2 or lower, your to-Strike is set to 6+, before other negative modifiers.
Border
Your Settlement's Reach is Range 1 or within Reach of another Player's Reach.
Break check
Taken by each Fatigued side's field every Skirmish, just before it gains its Fatigue token. Roll Morale (up to 5 dice, modified by Fatigue tokens); failures are casualties, but a Break check never triggers a Panic check. Unbreakable auto-passes.
Build
Construct Infrastructure (Industry action). → in context
Casualty
A retinue removed from the field — from an unsaved Strike or a failed Panic or Break check.
Charter
Found or upgrade a Settlement (Industry action). → in context
Cleave
On a natural 6 to Strike, you may roll an additional Strike die at your modified Strike value.
Condemned
An Envoy whose net Influence is -3 or less: it fails and you resolve that Domain's Condemn effect.
Contested
Territory that is within Reach of more than one player's settlement(s). Considered Controlled by all Players that have a Settlement within Reach.
Controlled
Territory that is within Reach of a single Player's Settlement(s).
Cost / Pay
Cost to perform an action, usually in Gold or Doubt.
Council Envoy
A free Envoy resolved in the Council Phase on the voted Domain; auto-Abstained, net Influence floored at 1.
Council Phase
Before Personal Envoys, all players vote on a Domain (clockwise; Host breaks ties). Each then sends a free Council Envoy of that Domain. Council Envoys auto-Abstain and their net Influence cannot drop below 1.
Deadly
On a natural 6 to Strike, increase the AP by -5. Can only be Parried or Recovered on a natural 6.
Deflect
-1 to Parry and Negate Riposte against this weapon's Strikes. (All Ranged weapons have Deflect.)
Demand Tribute
Coerce gold or concessions from another player (Prowess). → in context
Destroy Shield
On a natural 6 to Strike, target loses Shield attributes for the rest of the Battle.
Diplomacy
Free Envoy actions for forming and ending Treaties and Trade Agreements; resolved before Domain Envoys.
Domain
One of the four identities — Industry, Prowess, Cunning, Piety — raised by spending Domain points. Values 3/6/10 = Rising/Established/Sovereign Standing. → in context
Domain Point
Gained 1 per Rest Phase; spend to raise a Domain value by 1.
Doubt X
Gain X Doubt: each Doubt lowers your Public Order track by 1 when resolved.
Dual Wield
A Strike die that fails to Strike is rerolled once; the reroll can hit and can itself trigger a natural-6 effect. Dual Wield confers 2H (both hands on weapons — no Shield).
Dual-equip
Carry two weapons at once (e.g. melee + ranged). Granted by the Tiltyard, which also gives Unwieldy until its mastery removes it.
Edict
A scoring achievement / win path: reach a Sovereign Standing, complete a Monument, or fulfill a victory condition (Wonder, wealth, Vassalize, Living Saints, Last Standing). → in context
Endorsed
An Envoy that passes with 3+ net Influence, triggering its endorsed effect (and a Domain's Rising/Established/Sovereign endorsement).
Endurance
A side's stamina. Each side that fights loses 1 per Skirmish; at 0 it becomes Fatigued.
Envoy
The currency of actions: send an Envoy to perform an action during the Envoy Phase. Council Envoys act on the voted Domain; Personal Envoys are sent by Era progression.
Envoy Outcome
How a Sent Envoy resolves, by Net Influence: Condemned (<= -3), Failed (-3 < Net <= 0, gain Doubt 1), Passed (>= 1), Endorsed (>= 3). The per-domain effect at each band is given by ENVOY_OUTCOMES; an action's own endorsed bonus overrides the domain default.
Extort X
Take X from the stated source: the gold goes to you instead of its owner. → in context
Fail
An unsuccessful to Hit or to Save.
Failed
Net Influence 0 or less.
Faith X
Gain X Faith: each Faith raises your Public Order track by 1 when resolved.
Fall Back
A controlled retreat that ends the Battle with at least one retinue left — a partial success.
Fatigue Token
Each token is -1 to your Strike, Parry, and Recover rolls, to a maximum of 6+; and Morale -1 (uncapped). If your modified Morale is ever 7+, your army Routs. Tokens stack.
Fatigued
A side at 0 Endurance. Each Skirmish its field takes a Break check, then it gains a Fatigue token.
Field
Your retinues in play — front line (up to 10) plus reserve (up to 5). Casualties leave the field at once, lowering its count.
Heal X
At the end of each Skirmish, for every X casualties you took from Strikes, return 1 retinue to your Army.
Immune Panic
Automatically passes Panic checks.
Immune Unstoppable
Cancels the attacker's Parry from Unstoppable: this shield's -1 to Strike still applies, and the attacker's -1 to Parry does not.
Immune [keyword]
Cancels that keyword as it applies to you (e.g. Immune Unwieldy, Strain, Destroy Shield).
Improved Parry
Your Parry succeeds on 4+ instead of 5+.
Influence
The political currency of voting. Spend it to Support or Oppose Envoys. You gain it each turn from your Era, innate modifiers (trade partners, alliances, war), Pursuits, and Infrastructure. → in context
Influence X
An automatic +X (or -X) to an Envoy's net Influence from a Pursuit, Infrastructure, or Faction.
Initiative
Decides who Strikes first each Skirmish (higher first). Runs -2 to +2 (Ministry can raise the maximum to +3). At -2 or lower you Blunder.
Monument
A Domain's capstone Pursuit. Completing one scores its Edict and grants a powerful effect. → in context
Morale
How steady a retinue is when tested (lower is steadier; see the retinue table). Break and Panic checks roll it: a D6 per retinue in the field, up to 5 dice, each must meet its modified value; failures are casualties. If the modified value is ever 7+, the army Routs.
Move
Advance an Army up to its Speed in Territories (Prowess). → in context
Muster
Raise retinues into an Army, up to a Settlement's muster limit (Industry/Prowess).
Natural roll
The number on the die before any modifiers. Modifiers never change what counts as 'natural'.
Negate Riposte
The target's Parry can never Riposte this weapon's Strikes (a natural 6 Parry still cancels the Strike, but no counter-Strike follows).
Negate Tempered
Ignores Tempered: this weapon's AP can reduce the target's Save past 6+ (to auto-fail), defeating the Tempered floor.
Net Influence
The sum of an Envoy's starting Influence (1+ by Standing) and all Support, Oppose, and Influence X. The total sets the outcome: -3 or less Condemned, 0 or less Failed (gain Doubt +1), 1+ passes.
One Shot
May only be Equipped in the first Skirmish of a Battle. Requires a Tiltyard.
Oppose X
Spend X Influence to decrease an Envoy's net Influence.
Outlaw Country
Three uncontrollable territories in your starting region where Bandit Camps spawn. → in context
Panic check
Taken at most once per Skirmish by a side that suffered more than 5 casualties in that Skirmish, after it Strikes back. Roll Morale (up to 5 dice); Immune Panic auto-passes.
Parry
Roll a d6 to cancel a Strike before the Save on a 5+ (a natural 6 is a Riposte). -1 versus Unstoppable, versus ranged, and per Fatigue token (to a maximum of 6+).
Passed
Net Influence 1 or more.
Perform
When you Perform an action, immediately do the description of the action. Pay the action's normal Cost.
Personal Envoy
An Envoy you send in the Envoy Phase to perform an action; count and reach scale with Era.
Poison
A natural 6 to Save against this Retinue's Strikes fails.
Province
The collection of Contested Territory and Controlled Territory by your Settlement's Reach. → in context
Public Order
A track from -5 to 7, adjusted each turn by Faith minus Doubt; its band applies cumulative effects (see the Public Order table). → in context
Pursue
Build a Pursuit, spending its purchase cost and a Settlement ward (Industry). → in context
Range X
The amount of Territories you must move in order to get from your current position to the specific Territory.
Reach
How far a Settlement projects control, in Territories (by tier). → in context
Reach X
The Range X characteristic which determines what Territories you Control and are in your Province. → in context
Realm
The total collection of Regions.
Recoup
Regain the stated cost in gold after paying it. → in context
Recover
If a to-Save roll fails, roll a d6: a result of X+ saves the retinue. Worsened by Fatigue and Serrated, to a maximum of 6+.
Region
A collection of Territories of the same Type. → in context
Renown
The shared progress track. Gain 1 per Rest Phase; thresholds raise your Era (Ascension 8, Eminence 18, Zenith 30). → in context
Repair
Restore damaged Infrastructure or Settlements (Industry). → in context
Resolve
Attempt to Perform an action.
Riposte
If you roll a natural 6 on a Parry from a Melee Weapon's Strike, you Riposte: your opponent immediately takes a Strike from your equipped weapon. You can Riposte a Riposte.
Rout
The army breaks and leaves the Battle (you lose it). Whenever an army's modified Morale value reaches 7 or more, it Routs automatically.
Save
The defender's roll to avoid a casualty: roll a D6, add the weapon's AP (a negative) and the shield's Save bonus (a positive); the hit is saved on a result >= the armor value.
Seize the Initiative
Won by the roll-off at the start of the Battle — the winner of their last Battle adds +1 to the roll. You become the Attacker and gain +1 Initiative in the first Skirmish. Some Tactics and the Ministry monument also grant it.
Send an Envoy
Declare a Domain, spend 1 Envoy.
Serrated
-2 to Recover against this weapon's Strikes (worsens the defender's Recover roll by 2).
Shielded
A stacking -1 penalty to the Strike roll (to a maximum of 6+). Sources: a shield's -1 to Strike (ignored by Unstoppable unless the shield has Negate Unstoppable), some Tactics (the Ministry innate is immune to the Tactic source), and each Fatigue token.
Siege
Investing a Settlement with an Army to capture it; does not increment in Winter.
Skirmish
One round of a Battle, run through the numbered Battle steps; a Battle repeats Skirmishes until it ends.
Speed
An Army's movement allowance in Territories per Move action. → in context
Standing
Your tier in a Domain: Untested, Rising (3), Established (6), Sovereign (10). Sets max Influence per vote (1/2/3/4) and unlocks Domain effects. → in context
Steady
Initiative cannot be reduced by Tactics.
Strained
-1 Initiative every Skirmish (negated by Immune Strain). Does not gain Endurance in the Empire Phase.
Strike
A landed hit. Roll a D6, apply modifiers to the roll, and Strike on a result >= the to-Strike number. The target may then Parry, Save, and Recover.
Support X
Spend X Influence to increase an Envoy's net Influence.
Suzerain
The player who has Vassalized another; the vassal mirrors their Treaties.
Tactic
A choice both players make secretly and reveal together each Skirmish; it can shift Initiative and Strike rolls.
Tempered
Your armor Save cannot be reduced beyond a 6+.
Territory
1 Hex. → in context
Treaty
A standing agreement between players (e.g. Trade Agreement, Non-Aggression, Alliance) signed via Diplomacy. → in context
Unbreakable
Immune Break: does not take Break checks while Fatigued.
Uncontrolled
Territory that is not within Reach of any Player Settlement.
Unstoppable
Negate target shield's -1 to Strike, and impose -1 to Parry on the target.
Unwieldy
Initiative cannot be improved by Tactics.
Vassal
A player subordinated via Vassalization: their chartered settlements return to them, they mirror the Suzerain's Treaties, count as allied to the Suzerain, and cannot perform or be targeted by Diplomacy actions.
Vote
On each Envoy, every player in clockwise order from the starting player must Support, Oppose, or Abstain.
adjacent
Range 1.
next to
Range 2.
to-Strike number
The D6 result a retinue needs to Strike (see the retinue table; lower is better). Bonuses add to the roll; penalties and Fatigue tokens subtract.
within
Range 0.
