Action Mechanics

Sending an Envoy

Envoys are the currency of actions: to perform an action you send an envoy during the Envoy Phase. Sending costs 1 envoy, and you must be able to pay the action's cost before you send it. Declare the domain and the action — "I'd like to send a [Domain] envoy."

Resolving an Envoy

Your envoy begins with influence equal to your innate influence in its domain — Untested 0, Rising 1, Established 2, Sovereign 3 — plus any automatic Influence ±X modifiers (these come from your pursuits, infrastructure, and faction, and apply on their own; you never spend influence for them).

Then, clockwise from the starting player, every other player may respond:

  • Support X — spend X of your influence to raise the envoy's net influence.
  • Oppose X — spend X of your influence to lower it.
  • Abstain — spend nothing.

You only spend influence when you Support or Oppose. Abstaining is free, and you may let as much pass unopposed as you like — though other players may not like that. You cannot vote on your own envoy. Each player may spend up to 1 + their Standing in that domain on a single vote (Untested 1, Rising 2, Established 3, Sovereign 4); pursuits, infrastructure, and factions may raise this.

In practice this is not a round-the-table poll. The table simply asks "anyone support or oppose?" and resolves. The clockwise order is only there to settle the rare case where who-spends-first changes a decision.

Why this is the heart of the game: at Untested your envoy starts at 0, and you can't support it yourself — so it can only pass if another player chooses to support you. Influence is the currency of those favors. Unless players back each other, envoys fail; deciding whose actions to lift and whose to bury is the core of every turn.

Add everything together for the net influence, then read the outcome:

Net InfluenceResultEffect
-3 or lessCondemnedResolve that Domain's Condemn effect
0 or lessFailedGain Doubt 1
1+PassedPerform the action's Pass effect
3+EndorsedAlso perform the Endorsed effect

If a player has an effect that reduces a specific action's influence, that reduction is applied after the net influence is totaled, and can turn a would-be pass into a fail. That player may instead perform a different action targeting the same player or settlement.

When you perform an action, pay its cost (gold or doubt) and follow the pass effect. If endorsed, also perform the domain's endorsed effect.
